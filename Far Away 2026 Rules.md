DO# FAR AWAY 2026

**India's Biggest International Hackathon**

## Round 1 Rules & Submission Guidelines

Read carefully before submitting your project. Quality, originality and real-world execution are what we look for.


## 2. Building Philosophy

FAR AWAY is a builder-first hackathon.

We encourage:
- Coding
- AI-assisted development
- Open-source usage
- Rapid prototyping
- Creative engineering
- Shipping real products

> “The goal is not to write every line of code yourself. The goal is to build something meaningful.”

## 3. AI & Existing Projects

### Allowed
- All AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Cursor, Copilot, etc.)
- Open-source libraries and frameworks
- Existing codebases
- Previous projects that are significantly improved, redesigned or extended

### Not Allowed
- Submitting an old project without meaningful improvements
- Rebranding an existing project and presenting it as new work
- Copying another team's project
- Plagiarism

*Judges will evaluate the amount of demonstrated execution.*

## 4. Submission Requirements

### Mandatory
- GitHub Repository Link
- Project Submission (Presentation OR Video)



### Option 2: Video
- 2–5 minutes recommended
- Explain the problem, solution, features and show demo
- Judges should be able to understand the project entirely from the video

**Exceptions:** Complex projects may use longer presentations or videos on a case-by-case basis. Quality and clarity matter more than length.

## 5. Hardware Rules

- Physical prototypes are encouraged but not mandatory for Round 1.

### Mandatory for Hardware Projects
- PCB design files
- Schematics
- CAD designs (where applicable)

### Accepted Tools
- KiCad
- EasyEDA
- Altium
- Fusion 360
- SolidWorks
- Other industry-standard tools

**Note:** PCB and CAD work must be clearly shown. Projects with only Arduino boards and jumper wires without meaningful engineering work may be penalized.

## 6. GitHub Requirements

Every team must submit a GitHub repository containing source code, documentation, setup instructions and relevant files.

- Judges may review commit history where necessary.

---

## 7. Judging Criteria

- Innovation & Technical Depth
- Engineering Quality
- Real-World Impact
- Scalability
- Design & User Experience
- Execution Quality & Completeness

## 8. Verification

Judges may request:
- Source files
- GitHub access
- PCB/CAD files
- Demos
- Screenshots
- Development proof

Failure to provide requested material may result in disqualification.

## 9. Disqualification

Teams may be disqualified for:
- Plagiarism
- Fake demos
- Misleading information
- Misrepresentation of projects
- No meaningful implementation

## 10. Advancement to Round 2

- Top 100 teams qualify for the Delhi Offline Round.
- An additional 50 teams will be waitlisted.
- If a qualified team declines, waitlisted teams may be invited based on merit and rankings.

---

## What FAR AWAY Rewards

- Real products
- Strong engineering
- Creative use of AI
- Hardware with proper PCB design
- Technical depth
- Real-world impact

## What FAR AWAY Does Not Reward

- Idea-only submissions
- PowerPoint-only startups
- Fake demos
- Minimal-effort AI wrappers
